Building the In-House Campaign Engine

How ImmoScout24 Built a Scalable Creative Production Model

Role

Creative Director / Head of Visual Design

Company

ImmoScout24

Timeline

2023 - 2025

Team

Visual Design, Motion, Brand Strategy, Marketing, Project Management, External Production Partners

Overview

As ImmoScout24 scaled its marketing ambitions, the reliance on external agencies for campaign production was becoming a bottleneck - costly, slow and increasingly misaligned with the brand's evolving creative direction. As Creative Director and Head of Visual Design, I led the strategic shift toward an in-house campaign production model: building the team, processes, tools and creative frameworks needed to produce high-quality campaigns end-to-end from within the organization.

The Challenge

  • Heavy dependence on external agencies led to long lead times and high production costs
  • Creative output often lacked the depth of brand understanding that only an in-house team can provide
  • No scalable system existed for multi-format, multi-channel campaign production
  • The team lacked the structure and processes to handle end-to-end campaign delivery
  • Rapid growth in digital channels demanded faster iteration and higher volume of assets

Campaign: Einfach Zuhause / NICE

My Role

  • Defined the vision and strategic case for building an in-house production capability
  • Hired and developed a multidisciplinary team of designers, motion artists and producers
  • Designed the end-to-end creative workflow from brief to final delivery
  • Established quality standards, approval processes and production tooling
  • Led major campaign productions across TV, digital, OOH and social formats

Strategic Approach

The transition was built on three pillars: people, process and platform. We recruited specialists with both agency craft and in-house adaptability, developed modular production workflows that scaled across formats, and integrated AI-assisted tools to accelerate concepting, asset adaptation and quality control - all while keeping creative ambition at the center.

Out-of-Home - Berlin

Key Achievements

  • Reduced average campaign production time by over 40%
  • Significantly lowered external agency costs while increasing output volume
  • Delivered integrated campaigns across TV, digital, social and OOH entirely in-house
  • Established a motion and video production capability from the ground up
  • Integrated AI tools into the production pipeline for faster ideation and asset adaptation

AI in the Creative Process

A key part of the model was thoughtfully integrating AI into the creative production pipeline. From AI-assisted moodboarding and concept visualization to automated asset resizing and localization, we used emerging tools to accelerate work without replacing the creative judgment that makes campaigns distinctive. This became a competitive advantage - enabling the team to explore more ideas, faster, and deliver at a scale that would have been impossible through traditional workflows alone.

Conclusion

The in-house campaign engine demonstrates that with the right team, processes and tools, a brand can achieve agency-level creative quality at in-house speed and cost. More than an operational change, it was a cultural shift - one that gave the creative team greater ownership, deeper brand connection and the confidence to push ideas further.

Case Study

Calvin Klein

Rebuilding an Icon Through Integrated Creative and Social-First Storytelling

Role

Director Integrated Creative Content

Location

New York

Brand

Calvin Klein Global

Focus

  • Creative direction of global Calvin Klein campaigns across multiple platforms and channels
  • Creative concepts bridging fashion, retail environments and digital storytelling
  • Managing creative production and external partners, including photographers, filmmakers and agencies
  • Responsible for global brand consistency

Overview

When I joined Calvin Klein in New York as Director of Integrated Creative Content, the brand was at a pivotal moment.

Calvin Klein remained one of the world's most recognized fashion brands, but its creative expression had become fragmented. Different agencies, different teams and different creative approaches had led to an inconsistent brand experience across campaigns, digital channels and consumer touchpoints. While the business remained strong, the brand had lost some of the cultural relevance that had once defined it.

Rather than reinventing Calvin Klein, our ambition was to reconnect the brand with its core truth.

The Shift

Together with an exceptional in-house creative team, we shifted the focus back to what had always made Calvin Klein iconic: confidence, intimacy, individuality and a sense of effortless edge. The goal wasn't to create a new identity, but to reinterpret the brand's DNA for a new generation.

This transformation also marked a broader change in how creative work was developed. Instead of relying on fragmented external execution, we built a more integrated in-house creative approach that allowed brand, content, digital and social storytelling to work as one connected ecosystem.

#MyCalvins

One of the defining outcomes of this shift was #MyCalvins.

At a time when influencer marketing was still in its infancy, we helped pioneer a new way of building fashion campaigns. Instead of treating social media as an extension of traditional advertising, we made it the centre of the campaign. Artists, musicians, athletes, models and creators became authentic participants rather than conventional brand ambassadors. Their own communities became part of the storytelling, transforming audiences from passive viewers into active contributors.

Visual Language

The visual language evolved alongside this new approach. Inspired by the honesty and intimacy of photographers such as Tyrone Lebon, the imagery moved away from highly polished fashion advertising towards something more personal, spontaneous and culturally relevant. The result felt authentic, confident and unmistakably Calvin Klein.

What Changed

The campaign quickly became one of the most influential fashion campaigns of its time. Millions of people engaged with the hashtag, user-generated content became a central part of the brand experience and #MyCalvins helped establish many of the social-first marketing principles that are standard practice today.

My impact:

  • Helped reposition Calvin Klein as a culturally relevant, social-first fashion brand
  • Contributed to the creative transformation behind the globally recognised #MyCalvins platform
  • Helped establish a more integrated in-house creative model across brand, digital and social
  • Delivered globally consistent storytelling across multiple consumer touchpoints
  • Worked with world-class photographers, artists, talent and cross-functional teams to bring the brand's renewed vision to life

Looking Back

Looking back, what I'm most proud of isn't a single campaign image or celebrity collaboration. It's having been part of a team that fundamentally changed how one of the world's most iconic fashion brands connected with culture again. We didn't simply launch a campaign. We helped build a more integrated creative organisation and re-established a consistent brand voice that resonated across advertising, retail, digital and social.

Building an AI-Enabled Creative Studio

How I helped shift the Creative Studio from a campaign delivery team into a service model that supports brand quality, workflow efficiency, and AI-enabled ways of working across the Marketing Unit.

Role

Creative Lead / Service Model Design

Context

Creative Studio · Marketing Unit

Focus

AI Enablement, Brand Quality, Workflow Efficiency

Before

Campaign delivery

  • Manual production
  • Isolated workflows
  • Higher dependence on individual execution

After

AI-enabled service model

  • Agent-based workflows
  • Shared standards and governance
  • Scalable enablement and organizational leverage

Overview

Over the past weeks and months, my focus has shifted from individual campaigns toward a bigger operating-model change: helping the Creative Studio evolve into a more strategic, AI-ready service function. The goal is not only to produce better creative work, but to build a team and system that help the organization work faster, more consistently, and with less manual effort.

Context

The Marketing Unit is changing quickly. AI is becoming part of how work gets created, reviewed, and delivered. That means creative teams can no longer operate only as traditional production teams. They need to become more flexible, more scalable, and more capable of supporting others with standards, guidance, and reusable systems.

The Challenge

The existing model was too centered on campaign delivery and manual production. At the same time, new AI workflows were emerging across the organization, including agent-based work, AI-supported asset creation, and new enablement needs inside the Marketing Unit. The challenge was to move from isolated creative output to a more durable service model that could support quality, speed, and scale.

What I Did

I helped define a new direction for the Creative Studio:

  • Shifting the team from pure campaign delivery toward a broader service model
  • Building a clearer role for the Creative Studio in an AI-driven environment
  • Helping establish the AI Agent Hub for the Marketing Unit
  • Supporting the move from manual asset production to agent-based workflows
  • Creating stronger alignment around governance, quality, and creative standards
  • Enabling other teams to work more independently without losing brand consistency

What Changed

The Creative Studio is increasingly seen not just as a production team, but as a partner that helps the organization:

  • Move faster
  • Reduce repetitive manual work
  • Adopt AI in a practical and responsible way
  • Keep brand quality consistent
  • Build confidence in new workflows
  • Reuse systems instead of rebuilding everything from scratch

Why It Matters

This work matters because the future of design in a corporate environment is changing. AI does not remove the need for creative leadership. It raises the need for it. Teams need structure, standards, and clear operating models so they can use new tools without weakening quality or fragmenting the brand.

Result / Impact

The result is a Creative Studio that is better positioned for the future:

  • More strategic
  • More scalable
  • More service-oriented
  • More AI-ready
  • More useful to the wider organization

Instead of only delivering assets, the team is helping shape how creative work is done.

Proof Points

AI Agent Hub for the Marketing Unit

Move toward agent-based workflows

Shift from manual asset production to scalable systems

Improved brand governance and clearer decision paths

Stronger internal enablement and support models

Cross-functional alignment around new ways of working

Conclusion

This work is about more than efficiency. It is about helping a creative team evolve into the kind of function modern organizations now need: one that protects brand quality, enables others, and adapts intelligently to AI-driven change.

Case Study

Rebranding a Digital Market Leader

The ImmoScout24 Brand Refresh and Design System Transformation

Role

Design Lead / Creative Director

Company

ImmoScout24 (Scout24 Group)

Timeline

2024 - 2025

Collaborating Teams

Creative Studio, UX/Product Design, Engineering, Strategy, External Specialists

Overview

In 2025, ImmoScout24, Germany's leading real estate marketplace, reached a critical moment in its brand evolution. While the platform had grown significantly in scale and complexity, the visual identity had remained largely unchanged for several years. Product innovation, new services and changing user expectations required a brand that could better reflect the company's digital maturity and support future growth. As Design Lead within the Creative Studio, I initiated and led a comprehensive brand refresh and design system transformation to create a scalable brand architecture connecting product, marketing and corporate communication.

The Challenge

  • The existing visual identity no longer reflected the platform's digital maturity
  • Brand usage varied across product interfaces, marketing and corporate communication
  • The design language lacked a scalable system for different user journeys
  • Accessibility standards and regulatory requirements were becoming increasingly important
  • Internal teams lacked a centralized platform for brand guidance and assets

My Role

  • Initiated the brand refresh and identified the strategic need internally
  • Defined the creative direction of the new visual identity
  • Led collaboration between brand strategy, design, product and engineering teams
  • Guided the development of a scalable design system
  • Presented the vision to leadership and key stakeholders and led the conversation
  • Launched a new public brand portal

Strategic Approach

The project followed a structured process including a brand audit, defining a digital-first design direction, developing a modular design system and implementing the system across product, marketing and corporate communication.

Key Design Innovations

  • New typography system developed with typographer Alexander Roth
  • Accessible color architecture designed to meet current accessibility standards
  • Modular visual language supporting seekers, homeowners and real estate professionals

Brand Platform

To ensure the system could scale across teams, a centralized Brand Hub was launched on Frontify. The platform provides guidelines, assets, templates and collaboration tools, creating a single source of truth for the brand.

Company-Wide Rollout

  • Leadership presentations and stakeholder alignment
  • Workshops and internal communication for adoption
  • Integration with product and UX workflows

Conclusion

The ImmoScout24 brand refresh demonstrates how design leadership can align product, marketing and technology around a shared vision. The result is a scalable brand system prepared for the next generation of digital experiences.
